## About This Project

**Project name:** poker-apparel

**What it is:**
Designing poker apparel that directly influences opponents' behavior at the table, grounded in legitimate psychological research. Colors, text, patterns, and visual elements chosen to exploit documented human behavioral responses — one shirt to make opponents more risk-averse, another to encourage loose calls, etc.

**What success looks like:**
A wardrobe of poker shirts where each piece has a clear psychological thesis backed by research, with specific design choices (color, text, imagery, pattern) mapped to intended behavioral effects on opponents.

## Research Standards

1. **Strong bias to published, peer-reviewed research.** Mainstream behavioral psychology, color psychology with empirical backing, social psychology experiments with replication.
2. **Fringe theories are allowed** if they haven't been definitively debunked. Willing to entertain speculative or emerging research that hasn't been roundly refuted — label it clearly as speculative.
3. **No myth-busted pseudoscience.** If it's been thoroughly debunked by the literature, it's out. No exceptions.
4. **Cite sources.** When presenting research findings, reference the studies, authors, or meta-analyses so JK can evaluate credibility.
5. **Separate signal from noise.** Clearly distinguish between strong evidence, moderate evidence, and "interesting but unproven" when presenting findings.

---

## Project Standards

1. **Be accurate.** Get the science right. When unsure, say so.
2. **Explain your thinking.** When recommending a design element, connect it back to the research.
3. **Think like a poker player.** JK is a competitive player — recommendations should account for real table dynamics, not just lab conditions.
4. **Design for the table.** Consider lighting, viewing angles, distance, and the realities of how opponents actually see you during a session.
