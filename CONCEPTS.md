# Shirt Concepts: Cognitive Disruption & Underestimation

> 20 designs optimized for two simultaneous goals:
> 1. **Opponents underestimate you** — they call your value bets because they don't give you credit
> 2. **Opponents' decision quality degrades** — they call when they should fold AND fold when they should call
>
> Every concept lists the research mechanisms it targets and why they compound.

---

## Design Philosophy

The UCLA #2 jersey works because it stacks multiple mechanisms pushing the same direction — all passively, all below conscious awareness, all socially normative. These concepts follow the same principle: **layer multiple subtle effects rather than relying on one loud one.** Nothing should look like it's trying to be clever. The best psychological weapon is one nobody suspects is a weapon.

The dual goal (opponents both over-call AND over-fold) sounds contradictory, but it's really about **degrading decision quality generally.** An opponent operating on System 1 gut instinct instead of System 2 analysis will make errors in both directions — calling your value bets because "eh, I'm curious" and folding to your bluffs because "something feels off." The shirt's job is to keep opponents in that sloppy, instinct-driven mode.

## Concept 1: "The Buffet"

**Text:** "I'M JUST HERE FOR THE BUFFET"
**Color:** Warm khaki or sand base, brown/burgundy text
**Font:** Rounded, casual, slightly retro diner aesthetic
**Number:** Small "3" on the sleeve (incidental low anchor)

**Mechanisms:**
- Warmth/low competence signaling (Fiske SCM) — you're here for food, not poker
- Humor → System 1 shift (Strick et al.) — gets a chuckle, keeps table light
- Low anchor "3" processed incidentally (Tversky & Kahneman)
- Liking bias (Cialdini) — funny/relatable person gets less aggression directed at them

**Why it works at a final table:** Signals "recreational player who stumbled into a good run." Opponents call your river bets thinking "this guy doesn't know what he has."

## Concept 2: "The Retirement Fund"

**Text:** "MY RETIREMENT PLAN" with a small poker chip icon
**Color:** Light blue base (trust/calm), white text
**Font:** Handwritten style (warmth, authenticity — Schroll et al.)

**Mechanisms:**
- Blue → reduced perceived competitiveness (Geng et al. 2021; Nature HSSC 2025)
- Self-deprecating humor → System 1 shift + liking bias
- Implies you're gambling money you can't afford to lose → perceived as recreational/desperate player
- Handwritten font → warmth, personal, non-professional
- Loss-frame priming — "retirement plan" at risk activates loss concepts in opponents too

**Why it compounds:** Opponents think "this poor guy is in over his head" while simultaneously having loss-related concepts passively activated. They call your value bets (he doesn't know what he's doing) but may tighten up their own play (loss aversion primed).

## Concept 3: "The Lucky Shirt"

**Text:** "LUCKY SHIRT" in cracked/faded vintage print
**Color:** Faded soft green or light grey, worn-looking
**Font:** Distressed block letters, like an old thrift store find
**Detail:** Intentionally looks old, washed 100 times

**Mechanisms:**
- Implies superstitious thinking → low competence signal (serious players don't believe in lucky shirts)
- Vintage/worn aesthetic → low status signaling (opposite of Nelissen & Meijers luxury effect)
- Green → blends with felt, non-threatening, "invisible" at table
- Mere exposure to "LUCKY" primes luck-attribution in opponents (Zajonc 1968) — they attribute your wins to luck rather than skill, maintaining underestimation across multiple hands

**Why it compounds:** Even as you stack chips, opponents resist updating their model of you. "Lucky shirt" gives them a cognitive anchor to explain away your success without crediting your skill. They keep calling.

## Concept 4: "The Dad Joke"

**Text:** "I FOLD LAUNDRY" (with a small spade or club icon as the dot on the i)
**Color:** Heather grey base, navy text
**Font:** Simple sans-serif, standard t-shirt look

**Mechanisms:**
- Humor → System 1 (Strick et al.)
- "FOLD" processed semantically every time opponents glance at it (Stroop/obligatory processing) — primes the fold action
- Dad-joke energy → maximum warmth, minimum perceived competence
- Heather grey → neutral, forgettable, non-threatening

**Why it compounds:** Opponents laugh, relax, drop their guard. Meanwhile "FOLD" is being processed dozens of times per hour subliminally. They underestimate you AND have the fold concept passively activated. When you bet big, the primed "fold" concept and the "this guy's a joker" impression work together.

## Concept 5: "The Low Roller"

**Text:** "$2" in large, prominent display
**Color:** Powder blue base (like the UCLA jersey), gold/yellow text
**Font:** Bold, clean, jersey-style numbering
**Back:** Same "$2" large

**Mechanisms:**
- Direct replication of the UCLA #2 success pattern
- Anchoring with lowest possible dollar amount (Critcher & Gilovich)
- Dollar sign makes the numerical anchor explicitly financial (directly relevant to bet sizing)
- Blue → trust, low perceived competitiveness
- Jersey aesthetic → casual/sports fan, not poker professional

**Why it compounds:** This is the UCLA jersey distilled to its active ingredients. The "$2" is processed as both a low numerical anchor AND a status signal ("$2 player"). Opponents anchor their numerical cognition low and categorize you as a micro-stakes tourist.

## Concept 6: "The Dazzle Tourist"

**Visual:** Hawaiian-shirt-meets-dazzle-camouflage — tropical flowers and leaves rendered in bold black-and-white geometric dazzle patterns
**Color:** High-contrast black/white pattern with small pops of tropical color (coral, turquoise)
**Text:** None

**Mechanisms:**
- Hawaiian shirt aesthetic → warmth, vacation, non-serious (warmth-competence model)
- Dazzle pattern → disrupts opponents' ability to read physical tells (Stevens et al.; Hughes et al.)
- High contrast → captures attention, creates mild visual discomfort (Wilkins et al.)
- Cognitive load from complex pattern (Lavie 2005) — opponents burn mental resources parsing your shirt instead of calculating pot odds
- No text → nothing to consciously latch onto, just persistent low-level visual disruption

**Why it compounds:** Opponents see "tourist in a loud Hawaiian shirt" (underestimation) while the dazzle geometry is actively masking your physical tells and imposing cognitive load. Form AND function.

**Number:** Small "4" embroidered on sleeve

- Cream/blue → warm, non-threatening color combination

- Low anchor "4" on sleeve

**Why it compounds:** You become a character. "Larry the bowling guy" is so non-threatening that opponents can't reconcile the image with skilled play. The fake name is also a conversation starter (liking bias) and keeps opponents from Googling your actual results.

## Concept 8: "The Math Dropout"

**Text:** "2+2=5" in chalk-style font on a dark green background
**Color:** Dark green (chalkboard green), white text
**Font:** Chalk/handwritten style

**Mechanisms:**
- Directly signals mathematical incompetence → opponents assume you can't calculate pot odds
- Humor → System 1 shift
- The incorrect equation creates cognitive disfluency (Alter et al. 2007) — opponents' brains involuntarily correct it, burning processing cycles
- "2+2" anchors the number 4 (or 5) — low numerical anchor
- Dark green → blends with felt, non-threatening

**Why it compounds:** Every time an opponent glances at your shirt, their brain involuntarily processes "that's wrong, it's 4" — a tiny cognitive interruption, hundreds of times per session. Meanwhile they've categorized you as someone who can't do basic math. They will absolutely call your perfectly calculated value bets.

**Why it compounds:** The double meaning is invisible but potent. Opponents see a guy wearing a fishing trip souvenir and subconsciously file you as a "fish." Meanwhile, the calming nature imagery and warm color scheme keep them relaxed and willing to put chips in the pot against you.

## Concept 11: "The Fortune Cookie"

**Text:** "LUCKY NUMBERS: 2, 3, 5" (styled like a fortune cookie slip)
**Color:** White or cream base, red and gold accents (fortune cookie aesthetic)
**Font:** Faux-Chinese-restaurant typeface (the classic fortune cookie font)

- Humor/novelty → System 1 shift

**Why it compounds:** Opponents process three low anchors every glance, attribute your play to superstition rather than skill, and stay in a light/amused mental state. The fortune cookie framing makes the whole thing feel like a joke rather than a strategy.

## Concept 12: "The Dazzle Aloha"

**Visual:** Traditional aloha shirt pattern (hibiscus, palm leaves) but rendered in Op Art style — patterns that create optical illusion of movement
**Color:** Navy base with white/cream pattern (high contrast for dazzle effect but sophisticated enough for higher-stakes rooms)
**Text:** None

**Mechanisms:**
- Aloha shirt → tourist/vacation/warmth signaling
- Op Art movement illusion → persistent low-level visual disruption and cognitive load
- Pattern disrupts tell-reading (dazzle effect — Stevens et al.)
- Navy/white → more sophisticated than loud Hawaiian colors, works at higher stakes
- Visual discomfort from specific spatial frequencies (Wilkins et al.) → opponents unconsciously avoid looking at you, reducing their tell-reading opportunities

**Why it compounds:** The navy colorway makes this wearable at any stakes level without looking like a costume. The Op Art rendering takes a normal Hawaiian shirt and turns it into a passive tell-masking, cognitive-load-inducing tool. Opponents see "vacation shirt" and feel vaguely unsettled without knowing why.

## Concept 15: "The Puppy"

**Visual:** Large, adorable cartoon puppy face (think golden retriever or corgi)
**Color:** Light yellow or cream base, warm-toned illustration
**Text:** None, or small "good boy" text on back collar
**Detail:** The puppy's eyes are positioned at chest level — opponents make "eye contact" with the puppy

**Mechanisms:**
- Cute animal imagery triggers nurturing/caregiving response (Lorenz 1943, "baby schema" — Kdrn) → approach motivation, reduced aggression toward you
- Warmth signaling pushed to maximum — you are literally wearing a puppy
- Opponents feel subconsciously protective/friendly (Cialdini liking bias amplified)
- Puppy eyes at chest level → pareidolia-adjacent effect, opponents feel "watched" by a non-threatening entity, which is disarming rather than intimidating
- Light warm colors → non-threatening palette

**Why it compounds:** It is nearly impossible to play maximally aggressive poker against someone wearing a puppy. The nurturing response is involuntary and well-documented. Opponents soften their play without realizing why. They call your bets more (warmth = trust) and bluff you less (reduced aggression).

**Color:** Coral or salmon base, white text

## Concept 17: "The GPS"

**Text:** "HOW DO I GET TO THE BLACKJACK TABLE?" with a small confused-face emoji or question mark
**Color:** White base, blue text (trust + simplicity)
**Font:** Clean sans-serif, like a Google Maps query

**Mechanisms:**
- Lost tourist → maximum "wrong place" signaling
- Blackjack = house game, implying you don't understand player-vs-player competition
- Question format → warmth (asking for help = vulnerability = approachability)
- Humor → System 1 shift
- White base → openness, transparency (opponents may give your bets more credit as honest/face-value)

**Why it compounds:** You're literally wearing a sign that says "I don't know where I am." This is the most extreme underestimation play. Combined with the white = honesty association, opponents might paradoxically give your big bets credit ("he's too clueless to bluff") while still calling small/medium bets ("he doesn't know what he has"). This creates the ideal dynamic: big bluffs get through, value bets get called.

## Concept 18: "The Low Dazzle"

**Visual:** Geometric pattern inspired by Bridget Riley Op Art — undulating black and white lines that create apparent motion
**Color:** Black and white only
**Detail:** Pattern concentrated on torso/chest area where breathing tells are read
**Cut:** Slightly oversized/relaxed fit

**Mechanisms:**
- Maximum dazzle/tell-masking effect (Stevens et al.) — the undulating lines make it nearly impossible to detect subtle chest movements (breathing tells)
- Op Art motion illusion → persistent cognitive disruption every time opponents look at you
- Visual discomfort at specific spatial frequencies (Wilkins et al.) → opponents unconsciously minimize how often/long they look at your torso
- Black and white → no color-based intimidation, reads as "artistic" or "eccentric"
- Relaxed fit → casual warmth signal, hides body tension tells further

**Why it compounds:** This is the most functionally defensive design. It's not about what opponents think of you — it's about physically degrading their ability to read you. The Op Art pattern is a persistent irritant to their visual processing system, and the dazzle geometry masks the specific body regions where tells are most visible. The eccentric artistic look also contributes to underestimation.

## Concept 20: "The Cookbook"

**Text:** A fake recipe printed on the shirt:
```
RECIPE FOR DISASTER
1 cup overconfidence
2 tbsp bad reads
A pinch of tilt
Fold until done
```
**Color:** Cream/off-white base (recipe card aesthetic), dark brown or black text
**Font:** Typewriter/recipe-card style
**Detail:** Styled to look like an actual index card or cookbook page

**Mechanisms:**
- Extended text creates ongoing cognitive load — opponents keep re-reading it across the session
- "FOLD UNTIL DONE" primes fold action via obligatory processing (Stroop)
- Self-deprecating humor → warmth, likability, underestimation
- "Overconfidence" and "bad reads" prime opponents to think about THEIR OWN overconfidence and bad reads → may increase self-doubt and second-guessing
- Recipe/cooking framing → domestic, warm, non-competitive
- "2 tbsp" → low number anchor
- Multiple lines of text = multiple involuntary readings per glance = amplified priming

**Why it compounds:** This is a priming Swiss Army knife. Every ingredient in the "recipe" is a concept you want activated in opponents' minds: overconfidence (→ self-monitoring), bad reads (→ self-doubt), tilt (→ emotional awareness/anxiety), fold (→ action priming). All wrapped in a funny, warm, domestic aesthetic that screams "this person is not a threat."

## Top Picks: Highest Combined Effect

If forced to pick the concepts most likely to deliver measurable edge based on research confidence:

1. **#8 The Math Dropout** — Involuntary disfluency correction + incompetence signaling is a rare combination where both mechanisms are well-supported (Alter et al. + Fiske SCM)
2. **#4 The Dad Joke** — "FOLD" priming via Stroop effect is one of our highest-confidence mechanisms, and dad-joke warmth is universally disarming
3. **#20 The Cookbook** — Highest density of simultaneous priming targets, all research-supported, wrapped in the least suspicious packaging
4. **#5 The Low Roller** — Direct replication of the proven UCLA #2 pattern with the anchor made explicitly financial
5. **#14 The Astrology** — The persistent disfluency from the malapropism is a cognitive tax opponents pay every single glance
